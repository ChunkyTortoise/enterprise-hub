## üéì Understanding Industry Benchmarks

### Contribution Margin by Industry

| Industry | Typical Contribution Margin | Why? |
|----------|---------------------------|------|
| **SaaS** | 70-90% | Low incremental cost per customer, automated delivery |
| **E-Commerce** | 30-60% | High variable costs (shipping, returns, payment processing) |
| **Manufacturing** | 35-55% | Raw materials, direct labor, energy costs |
| **Services** | 50-80% | Labor-intensive but low material costs |
| **Marketplace** | 60-90% | Platform model, minimal COGS |

**Key Insight**: Higher contribution margin = higher operating leverage = faster profit growth after break-even

## üí° Advanced Use Cases

### Multi-Product Analysis
Run Margin Hunter for each product/service line:
- Compare break-even points across your portfolio
- Identify which products subsidize others
- Optimize product mix for profitability

### Pricing Strategy
Model different pricing scenarios:
- **Premium positioning**: +20% price, -30% volume (worth it if margin gain > volume loss)
- **Penetration pricing**: -15% price, +40% volume (viable if contribution margin stays positive)
- **Tiered pricing**: Analyze Starter, Pro, Enterprise tiers separately

### Cost Optimization
Test the impact of cost reductions:
- **Supplier negotiation**: Model -10% COGS impact on break-even
- **Process improvement**: Reduce variable costs by 15% via automation
- **Overhead reduction**: Lower fixed costs by 20% (remote work, outsourcing)
