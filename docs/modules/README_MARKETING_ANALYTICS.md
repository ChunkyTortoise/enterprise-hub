### 3. üë• Customer Acquisition Metrics

**Understand customer economics** with CLV and CAC analysis:

#### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- **Formula**: (Marketing Spend + Sales Costs) / New Customers
- **Inputs**: Marketing spend, sales costs, customers acquired
- **Output**: Cost to acquire one customer

#### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
- **Formula**: (Avg Purchase √ó Purchase Frequency √ó Lifespan) √ó Profit Margin
- **Inputs**: Purchase value, frequency, lifespan, margin
- **Output**: Total profit per customer over lifetime

#### CLV:CAC Ratio

- **‚ùå Poor**: <1:1 (losing money on customers)

### 5. üéØ Marketing Attribution Modeling

**Understand which touchpoints drive conversions** with 5 attribution models:

#### First-Touch Attribution
- **Logic**: 100% credit to first touchpoint
- **Use Case**: Measure top-of-funnel effectiveness
- **Example**: Social ad gets all credit for starting journey

#### Last-Touch Attribution
- **Logic**: 100% credit to last touchpoint before conversion
- **Use Case**: Measure bottom-of-funnel closers
- **Example**: Retargeting ad gets all credit for final conversion

#### Linear Attribution
- **Logic**: Equal credit across all touchpoints
- **Use Case**: Balanced view of full customer journey
- **Example**: 5 touchpoints = 20% credit each

#### Time-Decay Attribution
- **Logic**: More credit to recent touchpoints, less to earlier
- **Use Case**: Recency-weighted customer journey
- **Example**: Last touchpoint gets 2x credit of first

#### Position-Based Attribution (U-Shaped)
- **Logic**: 40% credit to first touchpoint, 40% to last, 20% split among middle touchpoints
- **Use Case**: Industry standard for e-commerce and SaaS - values both awareness and conversion
- **Example**: 5 touchpoints = First (40%), Middle 3 (6.67% each), Last (40%)
- **Distribution**: U-shaped curve emphasizing beginning and end of customer journey
- **Why It Matters**: Balances credit between top-of-funnel acquisition and bottom-of-funnel conversion, reflecting that both awareness and close matter equally

**Visual Output**:
- Bar chart showing conversion credit by channel
- Attribution breakdown table with percentages
- Model comparison for strategic decisions

**Example Journey:**
```
Customer Path: Social Ad ‚Üí Website ‚Üí Email ‚Üí Retarget ‚Üí Direct Purchase

Attribution Results:
- First-Touch: Social Media = 100%
- Last-Touch: Direct = 100%
- Linear: Each channel = 20%
- Time-Decay: Direct 40%, Retarget 30%, Email 18%, Website 8%, Social 4%
- Position-Based: Social 40%, Website 6.67%, Email 6.67%, Retarget 6.67%, Direct 40%
```

### Input Requirements

  - Optional columns: impressions, clicks, dates

### Calculations & Algorithms

#### ROI Metrics
```python
ROI (%) = ((Revenue - Spend) / Spend) √ó 100
ROAS = Revenue / Spend
CPA = Spend / Customers Acquired
CLV = (Avg Purchase √ó Frequency √ó Lifespan) √ó Margin
CAC = (Marketing Spend + Sales Costs) / New Customers
```

#### A/B Test Significance
```python
# Two-proportion z-test
z = (p_b - p_a) / sqrt(p_a(1-p_a)/n_a + p_b(1-p_b)/n_b)
p_value = 2 √ó (1 - norm.cdf(|z|))  # Two-tailed
significant = p_value < 0.05  # 95% confidence
```

#### Sample Size Calculation
- Based on desired confidence level (default: 95%)
- Power analysis: 80% power to detect effect
- Minimum: 30 samples per variant

## FAQ

### Q: Do I need marketing data in a specific format?
**A**: For automated features, CSV with columns: campaign, channel, spend, revenue, conversions. For manual calculators, just input numbers directly.

### Q: How accurate are the A/B test calculations?
**A**: We use industry-standard two-proportion z-tests (same math as Optimizely, VWO). Results match statistical software like R or Python scipy.

### Q: What if I don't have CLV data yet?
**A**: Use industry benchmarks to estimate (e.g., e-commerce: 3-year lifespan, 4 purchases/year). Refine over time with real data.

### Q: Can I track multiple campaigns at once?
**A**: Yes! Upload a CSV with all campaigns, or use the dashboard to filter by channel/date range.

### Q: Is my marketing data private?
**A**: Yes. All calculations happen locally in your browser/server. No data is sent to external services.

### Q: How do I know which attribution model to use?
**A**: Use all 5! Each tells a different story. First-Touch for awareness, Last-Touch for conversion tactics, Linear for balanced view, Time-Decay for recency, Position-Based for industry-standard e-commerce/SaaS attribution.

### Q: What's a good CLV:CAC ratio?
**A**: 3:1 or higher is healthy for most businesses. SaaS targets 3-5:1, e-commerce 2-4:1, B2B 5-10:1.

### Q: Can I use this for B2B marketing?
**A**: Absolutely! The metrics work for any marketing (B2C, B2B, SaaS, e-commerce, local). Adjust inputs to your business model.
